// About

The scientist, beyond the lab

A computational biochemist who believes that the deepest questions in science are also philosophical ones.

My academic path began in biochemistry and evolved through biotechnology toward the computational — a trajectory spanning molecular docking, quantum chemistry, biosimilar science, and statistical modeling. Before academia, I worked in pharmaceutical industry on monoclonal antibody development and original drug discovery.

I am drawn to problems where structure determines function, where models meet experiment, and where rigorous quantitative thinking makes the difference between a promising idea and a viable drug candidate.

Outside the research: pre-Socratic philosophy, Nietzsche, Popper's critical rationalism. And music — always music. Blues, Anatolian rock, metal, progressive.
